1. General Rules for Japanese Ticketing

The Japanese ticketing system is heavily biased toward domestic residents. **The biggest wall is verification:** most platforms require a Japanese phone number, a Japanese address for physical ticket delivery, and often reject foreign credit cards.

❌ **Warning:** Do NOT attempt to use fake credentials, as many venues now check IDs and you risk being denied entry.

2. Understanding the Ticket Lottery System (抽選)

The primary method for securing tickets is the **lottery (抽選)** system, often starting with Fan Club pre-sales. **This is your best chance.** If you are eligible, strictly adhere to the following:

  • **Fan Club Advantage:** Always aim for the Fan Club lottery, as it offers the highest probability of winning.
  • **Strict Rules:** Multiple lottery entries, duplicate accounts, or reselling tickets for profit are strictly forbidden and can lead to immediate account bans.
  • **Payment Timing:** Payment is usually required immediately after winning the lottery.

3. Using Official and Trusted Resale Platforms (リセール)

If you miss the initial lottery, **official resale platforms** are the safest route. These sites guarantee ticket authenticity and transfer rights, preventing scams.

  • **Trusted Sites:** Look for platforms officially partnered with the artist or venue (e.g., **チケプラリセール (Tixplus)**).
  • **Non-Official Risk:** **NEVER** buy tickets from unofficial third-party resellers, social media, or auction sites. These tickets are often fraudulent or tied to the original owner and will result in denied entry.

4. Recommended Agencies for International Fans

For international fans facing language and payment barriers, utilizing a **trusted Japanese ticket agency/proxy service** is the most reliable workaround.

These services handle the domestic application process (Japanese number, Japanese payment) on your behalf, significantly increasing your chances of successfully acquiring a ticket without violating rules.
